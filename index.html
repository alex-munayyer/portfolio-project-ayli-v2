Location Data in the Real Estate Industry

Footfall is perhaps one of the most important elements to consider for real estate agents when determining the value of each location. Location data helps real estate agents understand traffic metrics better and therefore make better marketing and finance decisions with it.

Foot traffic is perhaps one of the most important elements to consider for real estate investors when determining the value of each location. With location data, investors can pinpoint ‘hot properties’ long before others and therefore make relevant financial decisions before prices start going up.

2

How Location Data Can Help Hedge Fund Management

Hedge funds derive ‘actionable insights from real-time location data’ by understanding whether or not a property or a certain investment is seeing traction. More foot traffic usually means more demand, which means a better investment opportunity (usually). After that, all that’s left is a little due diligence.

Hedge funds derive actionable insights from real-time location data by keeping a close eye on the foot traffic public companies’ storefronts get. With this information, they are able to project sales for the quarter and determine how beneficial an investment in a given company would be.

3

Location Data & eCommerce

In the world of ecommerce, location data is nothing short of gold for marketers and suppliers. It helps businesses get a much better idea existing customers’ other interests, along with any potential customers exhibiting the same patterns as their older customers were at the time of purchase.  

With location-specific data, e-commerce platforms can get a better idea of buyer trends, motivation, traffic source, and implement geo-targeted marketing campaigns accordingly. Furthermore, they can target their physical storefront’s traffic to encourage them to convert to their e-commerce store – which is more convenient for customers and businesses alike.
